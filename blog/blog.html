A New Begining

11/27/2016

Aaaaaannnnnnnnnnndddddd... we're off

This is going to be an interesting experience for me. I am not usually a very open person online, but in order to keep myself on a schedule, I made myself a promise. At least once a week, I will post here.

This week was very productive in the DnD world. I started a new campaign, same old setting, with a new coat of paint. This time around, our adventurers are questing in the age of chaos Known as The War of Life. I have run two or three different Quest lines over my few years of dming. Normally, my large group quest is the Legend of The Chromatic Knights questline, where the heroes obtain legendary sentient swords, each tailored to a class, that they use to slay Asmodeus, Lord of Evil, and start a new age. My other campaign, which is the other one I am currently running, takes place after the WoL, in the age of Law, a time of competing gods, hidden evils, pirating, demigods, and peace in a broad sense. It's more of a “choose your own adventure,” but it generally revolves around the rise of The Mad God, the infighting between gods, and culminates with the destruction of the world barriers and the start of the war on Asmodeus. This new campaign is a mix of the two in some ways, and a new go at my setting. I'm going for a more amorphous central bad guy this time around. It's also the only official war setting I've run, so there is an extremely good chance that the party will get to see some battlefield action. They should get to see the fall of the dragons, the Aarakocra monks at their peak, and the start of the Viceroy Barony (I will explain my setting properly next blog.) We have (pending some new members) An Aarakocra Gunslinger as per Matthew Mercer’s archetype (see the card links), a Goliath Paladin, two Tiefling Warlocks (they are playing siblings), and an Elven Wizard. It should be quite a bit of fun, but I may have to mess with the original plotline I was planning. I’m definitely keeping the Percy cameo though.

In other news, Happy Thanksgiving! I’ve been a bit disillusioned about the holiday for a while, but that doesn’t mean you all can’t have fun! It also means we are T-4 weeks to Christmas! I love Christmas for exactly 0 of the religious reasons (barring some of the songs which are just a bit too deeply engrained for me to rebel against), but I do love “The Christmas Spirit.” Anyways, that’s for another blog.

Well that was rather ramble-y and unfocussed, I will make an attempt at better focusing my posts in future. Have a lovely day folks, and a genuine thanks for spending time.
